01 / The Eduba frame

Defense-vertical entry is a plumbing problem before it is a product problem.

We work on a simple idea. About 60% of what a research-forward consultancy ships into the defense vertical is traditional code and database work, scaffolded by federal-vendor compliance infrastructure. About 30% is rule-based logic. Around 10% is a genuine AI or alignment problem. The job is sorting those three into the right layers before anything is built, and attaching a veteran-owned SAM-registered partner to the infrastructure layer so the alignment and ML work compounds.

Applied to AE Studio's defense move, the map resolves quickly. CAGE and SAM registration, facility clearances, CMMC path, FedRAMP-adjacent hosting choices, and contractor-friendly contract vehicles are the infrastructure layer. Workflow compliance gates and federal-customer communication rules are the logic layer. Alignment research and ML build execution are the AE core. The partnership shape is the first three sitting with Eduba and the last one staying with AE, with ICM methodology on both sides.

That is the work we would propose to scope with the AE Studio leadership team and the practice leads in a first conversation.
